About Hot Wheels

Hot Wheels is a brand of die-cast toy cars introduced by American toy maker Mattel in 1968. It was the primary competitor of Matchbox until 1997, when Mattel acquired rights to the Matchbox brand from Tyco.

Hot Wheels has since become a hugely popular toy brand, with a wide range of vehicles and play sets available. The brand is known for its innovative designs and attention to detail, making it a favorite among collectors and children alike.
